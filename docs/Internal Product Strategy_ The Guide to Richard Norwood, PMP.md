# Internal Product Strategy: Richard Norwood, PMP (DBA)

This document defines how the personal brand (DBA Richard Norwood, PMP) introduces the doctrine, where it creates value, and where the handoff to Alabrida begins.

## Strategic Role

Richard's public role is the Guide. He helps a client understand their current commercial friction, creates an early operating rhythm, and proves the value of structure before a deeper infrastructure decision is required.

- Guide = client-facing, diagnostic, educational, and operational
- Navigator = the tone of the Guide role: calm, practical, and stage-aware
- Revenue Architect = the deeper design discipline underneath the work
- Alabrida = the escalation path when the client needs broader system design or more owned infrastructure

## Offer Ladder: The Three Tiers

### Phase I: Align (90-Day Diagnostic and Stabilization)
**Best fit:** Fragmented to Emerging businesses.
**Primary outcome:** clarity, prioritized fixes, and a first operating rhythm.
**Typical scope:**
- Commercial EKG and stage-by-stage audit
- messaging and proof cleanup
- basic routing, capture, and follow-up improvements
- first-pass dashboard or reporting setup

### Phase II: Build (Six-Month Orchestration)
**Best fit:** Emerging to Orchestrated businesses.
**Primary outcome:** a Managed Nervous System that improves visibility, handoffs, and follow-through.
**Typical scope:**
- tighter stage definitions
- microsite or website restructuring around the canonical journey
- nurture, review, and follow-up automation
- recurring operating reviews tied to buyer and customer signals

### Phase III: Command (Twelve-Month Transition)
**Best fit:** Orchestrated businesses moving toward a Unified state.
**Primary outcome:** a transition to long-term governance where the commercial engine is fully installed.
**Typical scope:**
- stronger governance and ownership rules
- tool rationalization and workflow simplification
- data model hardening and system documentation
- the final "Decision Fork" for the commercial estate

## The Tier 3 Decision Fork
At the completion of the **Command** phase, the client has reached the end of the Guide brand's primary journey. They have an installed commercial engine and must choose their long-term path:

1. **Stay Managed (Personal Brand):** The client chooses to remain a long-term partner of the Guide brand. Their commercial infrastructure remains hosted on the Guide's cloud infrastructure, and Richard manages the system as a fractional leader. This is ideal for owners who prioritize stability and want to avoid the overhead of technical ownership.2. **Migrate to Enterprise (Alabrida):** The client chooses to move to the Alabrida brand as an enterprise client. This involves migrating to a sovereign technical estate (custom infrastructure, complex data ownership, and broader system architecture).

## Pricing Logic

The Guide brand should stay easier to buy than the deeper architecture brand. Pricing bands below are internal planning assumptions, not public claims.

| Offer | Working monthly band | Commercial promise |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Tier 1: Align | ~$2,500 | clarity, prioritization, and first-system discipline |
| Tier 2: Build | ~$4,500 | stronger visibility, handoffs, and stage execution |
| Tier 3: Command | ~$8,000 | readiness for long-term governance or enterprise transition |

## Operating Guardrails

- **Architecture before tools:** define the rule set before recommending software.
- **Silent Referral:** The Guide brand does not publicly promote Alabrida. Alabrida is an internal escalation path only for clients who have reached Tier 3 maturity and require enterprise-level sovereignty.
- **Approachable Tone:** Keep the personal brand approachable: outcomes first, system logic second.
- **Infrastructure:** Use the Managed Nervous System as a bridge. For clients who "Stay Managed," the Guide maintains the technical stack on their behalf.
- **Escalation:** Move a client to Alabrida only when sovereignty, custom infrastructure, or complex data ownership becomes the real constraint.

## Persona Notes

The voice of this brand is practical and confidence-building. It should make structural work feel understandable, not intimidating.
