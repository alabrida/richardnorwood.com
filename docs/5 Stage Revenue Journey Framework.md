# 5 Stage Revenue Journey Framework

This is the canonical stage model used throughout the Richard Norwood doctrine. Every supporting concept in this folder should reinforce these five stages: Awareness, Consideration, Decision, Conversion, and Retention.

## Historical Translation

Earlier drafts used Learn, Education, Selection, and Impact. Those labels now map to Awareness, Consideration, Decision, and Retention. Conversion remains the fulcrum between the left and right side of the bowtie.

## Stage Overview

| Stage | Buyer question | Business responsibility |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Awareness | "Why should I pay attention to this problem now?" | become visible, relevant, and easy to understand |
| Consideration | "What kind of approach makes sense for me?" | teach, frame, and supply useful proof |
| Decision | "Is this the right choice, and is it safe to move?" | reduce risk, answer objections, and clarify fit |
| Conversion | "What happens if I say yes?" | turn commitment into a clean start with no confusing handoff |
| Retention | "Am I getting value, and should this relationship grow?" | protect adoption, renew trust, and create responsible expansion |

## Stage Notes

### Awareness

The goal is not immediate pressure. The goal is early relevance: clear positioning, accessible discovery points, and useful content that helps the right buyer recognize the problem.

### Consideration

This is where a digital presence needs to act like a guide, not a brochure. The buyer needs context, structure, and proof that helps them evaluate the shape of the problem and the fit of the solution.

### Decision

Decision is about risk removal. Transparent proof, practical FAQs, and simple next steps matter more here than louder claims.

### Conversion

Conversion is the bowtie fulcrum. A good sale can still become a bad experience if quote-to-cash, onboarding, and internal ownership are unclear.

### Retention

Retention includes the value-realization work earlier drafts described as "Impact." It covers onboarding, adoption, recovery, renewal, expansion, and advocacy.

## Operating Notes

- Information Fusion supports all five stages by keeping useful context visible.
- The stage model is buyer-facing; the Unified Commercial Engine is the operating system that supports it.
- Strong performance in one stage does not compensate for weak handoffs between stages.
